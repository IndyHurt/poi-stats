Percent change is the relative change in total kilometers between the value reported by the World FactBook and the value reported by our summary stats calculations. Our summary stat calculations are based on a sum or all road lengths in OpenStreetMap summarized by country. The road types in particular that comprise this total include the following: motorways, primary roads, secondary roads, tertiary roads, trunks, residential roads, unclassified roads, and service/other roads.

Percent Change = (Current OSM Road Length - World FactBook Road Length / World FactBook Road Length) * 100

Growth rate is the average change each year. Of course, change is not constant from year to year, but this measure gives us one view into the data and community activity in each country.

Growth Rate = ((Current OSM Road Length - World FactBook Road Length / age of World FactBook data in years) / World FactBook Road Length) * 100

Road lengths obtained from statistics_2016_09_05-14_05_01.sqlite, summary stats for Mapzen Valhalla tiles.

World FactBook road lengths obtained from their website: WFB Road Lengths

Malaysia, Brazil, Iceland, Burkina Faso, Uganda, and Bulgaria were deamed uncomparable. See notes on WFB Road Lengths page for details.

Comparison is based on growth rate and percent change since the year the World FactBook data cites their data. OpenStreetMap road length calculations are current whereas the values reported by the World FactBook are up to 17 years old.
